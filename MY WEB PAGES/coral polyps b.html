CORAL POLYPS

Coral polyps are small living organisms in salty waters that exist in million of colonies.
Coral polyps colonised the flancks of volcanic Islands in the sea or oceans.
This is because of the abundant plunctons and the indented nature of the Islands.
Polyps breed from the indented features, also reduced numbers of fish in such waters help in multiplication of these polyps since there is reduced threat of predators which could have fed on them.

Coral landforms

These are landforms on the sea and oceans formed as a result of death, deposition and compaction of polyps.

  • 1. Coral reefs
  • 2. Barrier reefs
  • 3. Fringe reef
